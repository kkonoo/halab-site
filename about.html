About us

Bridging genomics and medicine.

We aim to uncover the genetic and epigenetic mechanisms underlying complex human diseases, and to translate those discoveries toward precision healthcare.

Our Mission

We aim to uncover the genetic and epigenetic mechanisms underlying complex human diseases. By integrating large-scale multi-omics datasets with advanced computational and systems-biology approaches, we seek to understand how genetic variants shape molecular regulation and contribute to disease risk.

Our Vision

Our research is grounded in basic medical science, but we aim to extend our findings toward medical applications and precision healthcare. By bridging genomics and medicine, we hope to identify novel biomarkers and therapeutic targets for human diseases.

Research Focus

Where we concentrate our efforts

  • Multi-omics data integration — genomics, transcriptomics, and epigenomics (ATAC-seq, RNA-seq, single-cell multiome).
  • GWAS integration — linking complex-trait variants to regulatory elements and their target genes.
  • Immune-related & chronic diseases — using the kidney as a model system to study genetic regulation, with broader implications for other chronic diseases.

Our Values

We value an open and collaborative environment where curiosity, creativity, and persistence drive discovery. We welcome students and researchers who are eager to learn, explore, and contribute to a vibrant academic community.
